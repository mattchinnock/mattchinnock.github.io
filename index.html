Portfolio

How do I manage projects?

1
Build the team

Ensure the team consists of people with the right skills; a single Product Owner that is responsible for making decisions at a macro scale, a Scrum Master to organize the team and help solve logistical and operational blockers, an Architect to make technical decisions and handle development at an application level, Designers to create wireframes, mockups and designs with a focus on user experience and well thought out UI, Developers to build and bugfix, and QA to test recently developed features before they make it into a release.

2
Create the Backlog

Define the overal project requirements with the Product Owner. These are organized into “Epics”. Epics represent large, mostly self-contained blocks of functionality that will be broken down into smaller subtasks, or “Issues”. Each issue is a well defined piece of work, with a clear discription, acceptance criteria and test criteria that can easily be picked up and worked on with minimal explaination needed. These issues are placed in a list, in order of priority decided by the Product Owner. At the end of each Sprint, the backlog is revisted.

3
Establish a workflow

Establish the various stages of the development lifecycle - the length of Sprints, shared tools, design practices to which each team member should adhere, where code is developed, how new features are tested, how the final product is deployed and how information needs to be documented. Design a workflow that is repeatable and accessible to the entire team. All team members should be comfortable with the process from conceptual design to completion.

4
Plan, build, test, deploy. Repeat.

Following Agile best practices, the team goes through a section of the backlog to assign story points to issues. Every team member has input, ensuring that the issues selected for a Sprint are reasonable in terms of effort, and each issue is clearly defined. Every day during development, the team meets briefly to discuss it's progress and any potential problems. After the Sprint has finished, work is tested by the QA team before being deployed. Each sprint should leave the Product owner with functional, usable code.

I work primarily in the Product Manager or Lead Developer roles, overseeing technical decisions with input from architects, UX designers, graphics designers and the Product Owner. Interested in working with me? Contact me direcly via email.
